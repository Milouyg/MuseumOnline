History

BEGIN

The government began to establish a national museum in The Hague in 1798. It housed more than 200 paintings and historical objects, some from the stadholder collections and some from (disbanded) national institutions, such as the VOC. partly from (disbanded) national institutions, such as the VOC.

Paintings

Here the most important paintings of the city were brought together and in 1809 the Royal Museum on the top floor of the palace. With William I as the new king of 1813, the museum moved as the "National Museum".

PIERRE CUYPERS

Many collections were housed. Museums were renovated all over Europe, Pierre Cuypers, had created a design in historical style for the Rijksmuseum.

END RESULT

During the last renovation (2003-2013), the building received back its original structure by Cuypers. It now forms one chronological story together: the story of Dutch art and history.
